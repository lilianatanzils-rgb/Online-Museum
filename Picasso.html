About Pablo Picasso

Pablo Picasso (1881–1973) was a Spanish painter, sculptor, and co-founder of Cubism. His creativity, innovation, and ability to reinvent his style made him one of the most impactful artists in modern art.

Throughout his long career, Picasso created over 20,000 artworks, constantly pushing boundaries and redefining artistic expression.

Famous Works

Guernica (1937)

His powerful anti-war masterpiece, painted during the Spanish Civil War.

Les Demoiselles d’Avignon (1907)

A revolutionary work that led to the beginning of Cubism.

The Weeping Woman (1937)

A dramatic portrait symbolizing pain and suffering during war.
